Essential Japanese Phrases

こんにちは (Konnichiwa)

Hello (daytime greeting)

ありがとうございます (Arigatou gozaimasu)

Thank you (polite)

すみません (Sumimasen)

Excuse me / I'm sorry

お願いします (Onegaishimasu)

Please (when requesting something)

英語が話せますか？ (Eigo ga hanasemasu ka?)

Do you speak English?

これはいくらですか？ (Kore wa ikura desu ka?)

How much is this?

トイレはどこですか？ (Toire wa doko desu ka?)

Where is the toilet?

おいしい！ (Oishii!)

Delicious! (Great compliment for food)

Travel Tips

Transportation

  • Get a Suica or Pasmo IC card for easy train/bus travel
  • Shinkansen (bullet trains) require separate tickets
  • Google Maps works well for transit directions
  • Trains stop running around midnight
  • Taxi doors open automatically - don't touch them!

Etiquette

  • Bow slightly when greeting
  • Remove shoes when entering homes and some restaurants
  • Don't eat while walking
  • Point with open hand, not finger
  • Don't tip - it's considered rude
  • Use both hands when giving/receiving items

Packing Essentials

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Layers for variable spring weather
  • Portable umbrella
  • Small coin purse (Japan uses lots of coins)
  • Handkerchief (many restrooms lack paper towels)
  • Universal power adapter (Japan uses Type A/B plugs)
  • Portable WiFi or SIM card

Money & Shopping

  • Carry cash - many places don't accept cards
  • 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards
  • Tax-free shopping available for purchases over ¥5,000
  • Don't count money directly into someone's hand
  • Place money on the tray provided at registers

April Weather

  • Daytime: 15-20°C (59-68°F)
  • Nights: 10-15°C (50-59°F)
  • Possible rain - pack a light waterproof jacket
  • Cherry blossoms may still be visible early in your trip
  • Golden Week begins late April - expect crowds
